Day 1
Choose & Break Down Your Standard
Pick your 7th grade objective, decode the language, and write a student-friendly "I can..." goal.
Planning page collected
Step 1
Select & copy your standard
Choose one standard from the class list. Write the full standard number and text on your planning page.
Step 2
Find the key words
Identify the important math verbs (what students do) and nouns (what they work with). List them separately.
Step 3
Put it in your own words
What is this standard actually asking students to do and understand? Rewrite it plainly — no jargon.
Step 4
Identify prior knowledge
What would a student already need to know to learn this? Think about vocabulary and earlier skills.
Step 5
Spot the tricky parts
Where might students get confused or make mistakes? This will help you plan how to teach it.
Step 6
Write your "I can..." goal
Turn your standard into a student-friendly learning goal using the sentence starter on your planning page.

Day 2
Plan Your Lesson
Use your Day 1 page to choose your teaching method, visual, build a timed outline, plan student notes, and assign team roles.
Planning page collected
Step 1
Review your Day 1 page
Look back at your standard, "I can..." goal, and tricky parts. These will guide every decision you make today.
Step 2
Choose your teaching method
How will you introduce and explain the concept? Pick one main approach — real-world hook, worked example, visual model, or your own strategy.
Step 3
Plan your visual or model
What will students see or interact with? A number line, table, diagram, graph? Sketch or describe it.
Step 4
Write your lesson outline
Map out the order of your lesson with time estimates. Aim for 8–10 minutes total.
Step 5
Plan student notes
What do you want students to write down? Decide what goes in their notes and how it should be organized.
Step 6
Assign team roles
Who teaches which part? Who manages the visual? Who watches the time? Write it down.

Day 3
Build Slides & Teacher Notes
Take your Day 2 outline and turn it into 4–6 real slides. Every slide needs content AND speaker notes — what you'll actually say.
Planning page collected
Step 1
Review your Day 2 outline
Each section of your plan should become one or two slides. Map it out before you start building.
Step 2
Plan your slide order
You need at least: a title slide, your learning goal, your explanation, a guided practice problem, and a wrap-up.
Step 3
Write each slide's content
Keep slides clean — key words, visuals, and examples only. Students should be listening to you, not reading walls of text.
Step 4
Write speaker notes for every slide
Notes are what you'll say out loud. Write in full sentences. Include how you'll introduce the slide and questions you'll ask.
Step 5
Plan your student handout
What will students write or fill in during your lesson? Design a simple notes page that matches your slides.
Step 6
Do a quick read-through
Read your notes aloud. Do they sound natural? Fix any gaps where you're not sure what to say.

Day 4
Design the Student Activity
Build the activity students will do during your lesson. Choose any format that gets them practicing the skill from your "I can..." goal.
Planning page collected
Step 1
Choose your format
I Do/We Do/You Do, partner challenge, card sort, math game, real-world task, or your own idea. Pick what fits your concept.
Step 2
Write your problems or tasks
Create 3–5 problems that connect to your "I can..." goal. At least one should address the tricky part from Day 1.
Step 3
Write student instructions
Step-by-step directions students can follow on their own — as if you won't be there to explain.
Step 4
Plan materials & setup
List everything the class needs. Think about how long setup takes.
Step 5
Write your answer key
Work through every problem and record the answers. You'll need this for the debrief.
Step 6
Plan your debrief
How will you wrap up the activity in 1–2 minutes and connect back to the "I can..." goal?

Day 5
Build Your Assessment
Create a check for understanding aligned to your "I can..." goal. Choose any format. Optional homework included.
Optional day
Step 1
Choose your format
Exit ticket, short quiz, Google Form, whiteboard check, reflection prompt, or your own idea. Pick what fits your lesson and time.
Step 2
Write your questions or prompts
Create 2–4 questions that connect directly to your "I can..." goal — not just any 7th grade math.
Step 3
Write your answer key
Work through every question and record the answers. You'll need this to check student work after your presentation.
Step 4
Plan the logistics
How will students receive it? How long will it take? When in your lesson does it happen?
Step 5 — Optional
Design the homework
Create original problems OR find a real-world application. Either works — your choice.
Step 6 — Optional
Write homework instructions
Clear student-facing directions — how many problems, what to show, and how it connects to the lesson.

Day 6
Rehearse & Final Touches
Run through everything, time your lesson to 8–10 minutes, and fix any last issues before presentation day.
Self-check — not collected
Step 1
Do a full run-through
Present start to finish as if the class is watching. One person times, one person teaches. Then switch and debrief.
Step 2
Check your time
Target 8–10 minutes. A little over is fine — your teacher will give some grace. If you're under 8, slow down and add more explanation.
Step 3
Run through the activity
Practice explaining the activity instructions out loud. Know exactly how to set it up and transition into it.
Step 4
Check every slide
Read each slide and its speaker notes. Fix typos, unclear wording, or slides that feel incomplete.
Step 5
Check all your materials
Student handout, activity materials, assessment, answer key — do you have everything?
Step 6
Decide if you're ready
What's your biggest strength? What's the one thing you'd still fix if you had more time?

Day 7
Presentation Day — Let's Go!
Today you teach. Every team presents their mini-lesson, leads their activity, and gives their assessment. Target: 8–10 minutes.
Presentation day
If you are presenting
✓ Speak clearly and loud enough for the whole class
✓ Use your speaker notes — don't read off the slides
✓ Aim for 8–10 minutes — a little over is okay
✓ Explain activity instructions before you start
✓ Both team members must present equally
✓ Give the assessment and collect it before you sit down
✓ Stay calm — you've practiced this. You've got it!
If you are watching
👁 Eyes on the presenters — no phones or other work
📝 Fill out your feedback form for every presentation
🤝 Participate in the activity when it's your turn
✏️ Complete the assessment honestly — it's a real check
🤫 No side conversations while a team is presenting
✋ Raise your hand to ask questions — wait until invited
⭐ Be the audience you'd want when it's your turn
📋 Feedback forms are collected at the end of class and count as a participation grade. Complete every section honestly and specifically for each team.
